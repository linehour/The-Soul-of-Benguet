Before You Go

Travel Tips

Everything you need to know for a safe, memorable, and responsible highland adventure.

Best Time to Visit

Benguet's highland climate varies — plan ahead for the experience you want

❄️
Cool Season
Nov – Feb

Best weather — crisp air, clear skies, and sea-of-clouds at Mt. Pulag. Ideal for hiking and festivals.

⭐ Peak Season
🌸
Flower Season
Mar – May

Atok blooms with sunflowers and roses. Warm days but still pleasant. Strawberry harvest peaks in March.

🌧️
Rainy Season
Jun – Sep

Heavy rains can affect mountain roads. Trails may be slippery. Check typhoon advisories before travel.

🍂
Harvest Season
Oct – Nov

Vegetable and rice harvest time. Vibrant markets, cultural gatherings, and the grand Adivay Festival in November.

Essential Travel Tips

Read these before heading to the highlands

1
🚌

Getting There & Around

Benguet is accessible from Manila via several bus lines. From La Trinidad (the provincial capital), jeepneys and tricycles connect to most municipalities. Roads can be narrow and winding — allow extra travel time, especially for remote areas.

✓ Victory Liner (Manila → Baguio, ~6hrs) ✓ Joy Bus Premium (overnight options) ✓ Jeepneys within La Trinidad ✓ Rent 4WD for mountain routes ✓ Kennon Road, Marcos Hwy, or Naguilian Road entry
2
🧥

What to Pack

Benguet's highlands can drop to 5°C or below at peak season, especially at Mt. Pulag's summit. Layering is essential. Weather can shift dramatically in a single afternoon — always be prepared for both sun and rain.

✓ Thermal or fleece jacket ✓ Light rain jacket or poncho ✓ Waterproof hiking boots ✓ Sunscreen (high altitude = stronger UV) ✓ Reusable water bottle ✓ Headlamp for early treks
3
🛡️

Safety & Hiking Reminders

The mountains of Benguet are beautiful but demand respect. Always inform someone of your itinerary and expected return time. For major trails like Mt. Pulag, advance booking and DOT-registered guides are mandatory — not optional.

✓ Hire DOT-accredited local guides ✓ Register at the DENR trailhead office ✓ Never hike solo on remote trails ✓ Carry first aid essentials ✓ Check weather forecasts 24–48 hrs ahead ✓ Bring emergency whistle and signal mirror
4
📋

Permits & Advance Booking

Several destinations in Benguet require permits obtained days or weeks in advance. Mt. Pulag, in particular, limits the number of visitors per day to protect its fragile ecosystem. Walk-ins are rarely accommodated for major peaks.

✓ Mt. Pulag: DENR-CAR permit required ✓ Book at least 2–4 weeks ahead for peak season ✓ Mt. Ulap: register at Ampucao Barangay Hall ✓ Kabayan Mummy Caves: local tourism office permit
5
🌿

Responsible & Eco-Tourism

Benguet's highlands are among the most biodiverse and culturally sacred landscapes in the Philippines. Every visitor has the responsibility to preserve what makes Benguet extraordinary for generations to come.

✓ Pack out all your trash (no trash bins on trails) ✓ Do not pick wildflowers or uproot plants ✓ Stay on designated trails ✓ No camping fires outside designated zones ✓ Use biodegradable toiletries ✓ Buy local — support indigenous artisans
6
🙏

Respecting Local Culture

Benguet is home to the Ibaloi, Kankanaey, and Kalanguya indigenous peoples. Their customs, sacred sites, and rituals deserve deep respect. Ask before photographing locals, especially during ceremonies. Learn a few Ibaloi or Kankanaey greetings — the locals will love it.

✓ Ask permission before taking photos ✓ Dress modestly in sacred or ritual spaces ✓ Do not enter burial grounds uninvited ✓ Respect "cañao" ceremonies — they are private rituals ✓ Learn: "Oka" = Thank You (Ibaloi)
7
📱

Connectivity & Money Matters

Signal can be weak to non-existent in remote municipalities. Download offline maps before you go. Most local markets and eateries are cash-only — withdraw before leaving La Trinidad or Baguio City, as ATMs are scarce in the highlands.

✓ Download Google Maps offline (Benguet region) ✓ Bring enough cash — no ATMs past La Trinidad ✓ Globe tends to have better coverage in upland areas ✓ Power banks are essential on multi-day treks
8
💊

Health & Altitude Awareness

Mt. Pulag's summit sits at 2,922m above sea level. Altitude sickness (headache, nausea, dizziness) is real. Acclimatize gradually and descend immediately if symptoms worsen. Drink plenty of water and avoid alcohol before high-altitude climbs.

✓ Acclimatize in La Trinidad (785m) first ✓ Stay hydrated — drink 3L/day while trekking ✓ Carry paracetamol and oral rehydration salts ✓ No alcohol 24hrs before summit attempt ✓ Consult a doctor if you have heart or lung conditions

📋 Quick Reference Guide

At-a-glance facts for your highland trip

Category Details
🏙️ Capital / Hub La Trinidad, Benguet — main transport and accommodation hub
🌡️ Temperature 5°C – 18°C (peak season); 12°C – 25°C (summer); near 0°C at Mt. Pulag summit
🚌 Bus Lines Victory Liner, Dagupan Bus, Genesis Transport, Joy Bus — all route through Baguio City

🗣️ Languages Filipino, English, Ibaloi, Kankanaey — most tourism staff speak English
💱 Currency Philippine Peso (PHP). Budget ₱1,500–₱3,000/day for a mid-range highland trip (excluding transport).
